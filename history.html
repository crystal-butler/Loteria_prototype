  • Loteria is a mexican game of chance, similar to bingo but using images from a deck of 54 cards rather than plain numbers.

  • Loteria is spanish for 'lottery'.

  • The game was brought to new spain (mexico) in 1769. originally a pastime of the upper classes, it eventually became a tradition at mexican fairs.

  • Images have assigned names and numbers.

  • As shown on the antique tablas (playing boards) on the right, randomly assorted images matching the card deck are laid out in a grid.

  • The Cantor, or caller, calls out the cards in turn as players mark them off on their tablas.

  • The first player to match any of the winning patterns calls 'loteria!,' and wins the game.
